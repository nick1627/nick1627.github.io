Orbits in 3D

In 2D, the parameters commonly used to specify an orbit are:

  • the semimajor axis \(a\)
  • the eccentricity \(e\)






Using these three angles:

  • the longitude of ascending node \(\Omega\)
  • the inclination \(i\)
  • the argument of periapsis \(\omega\)
the orientation of an orbit can be specified in 3D space.
